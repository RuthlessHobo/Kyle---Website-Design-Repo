# Stannect Vertical Presets

Starting systems for Kyle's actual client verticals and ventures, so a build begins from something specific instead of a blank prompt. Each preset names the anchor to start from, the page's real job, the copy voice, and the conversion priority. **These are starting points — customize per client.**

> Pull the client's real brand (logo, colors, photos) when it exists and extend from it. Where a client has none, use the anchor tokens in `aesthetic-directions/direction-templates.md`.

---

## 1. Home Services — Roofing / Construction / Remodelers

- **Anchor:** **A. Grounded Industrial** (or **B. Warm Service** if the client wins on approachability over ruggedness).
- **Page job:** capture the lead *fast* — book an inspection / send storm-damage photos / get a quote. Speed-to-lead is the metric.
- **Hero:** proof-first — real job-site or before/after photo + a plain outcome headline ("Storm damage handled in days, not weeks") + one dominant CTA.
- **Must-have sections (in priority order):** trust strip (licensed/insured/reviews/years) → the offer in plain terms → real work gallery (their photos, not stock) → simple process (only number it if it's truly sequential) → financing/insurance help if relevant → reviews near the CTA → fast lead-capture (short form + click-to-call).
- **Copy voice:** direct, confident, plain — a crew that's good at its job and respects the homeowner's time. No corporate filler.
- **Conversion:** one primary action per section; short form or click-to-call; verify the form wires into GHL. A beautiful disconnected form is a dead lead.
- **Trust signals to surface:** license #, insurance, warranty/guarantee, Google rating, real names/faces, service area.

## 2. Home Services — HVAC

- **Anchor:** **A. Grounded Industrial**, leaning on the **hot/cold duality as real structure** (cool blue / warm amber expressing the two sides of the service — not decoration).
- **Page job:** emergency/seasonal lead capture + maintenance-plan signups.
- **Hero:** outcome + urgency ("Comfortable by tonight — same-day service"), real tech/van photo, click-to-call prominent.
- **Sections:** services split by heat/cool, emergency CTA, maintenance plan, financing, reviews, service area, capture.
- **Copy voice:** reassuring + responsive; emphasize speed and reliability.
- **Conversion:** click-to-call above the fold (mobile-first — these are urgent mobile searches); maintenance-plan as the secondary path.

## 3. Rental Operators — Jet Ski / Boat / Watersports

- **Anchor:** **C. Marine / Leisure** (or **D. Premium Build** for luxury charters).
- **Page job:** drive bookings; communicate availability and the experience.
- **Hero:** full-bleed real water/experience photo + bold booking action ("Check availability") over it.
- **Sections:** the fleet/options with real photos + clear pricing → how it works (genuinely sequential → numbering OK) → what's included / requirements → location & hours → reviews/photos from real customers → booking.
- **Copy voice:** energetic, aspirational, frictionless — sell the day, not the equipment.
- **Conversion:** make availability/booking obvious and fast; reduce form friction; surface deposit/cancellation clearly to cut back-and-forth.
- **Metric framing (ties to Stannect dashboard):** utilization and repeat bookings — design should push repeat/referral (capture email, offer return discount).

## 4. Rental Operators — Equipment Rental

- **Anchor:** **A. Grounded Industrial** (machine yellow/black, hi-vis, steel).
- **Page job:** quote/reservation requests by equipment type.
- **Hero:** the gear in real use + "Reserve [equipment] today."
- **Sections:** categories with specs + day/week pricing, availability/quote request, delivery info, terms, reviews.
- **Copy voice:** practical, spec-forward, reliable.
- **Conversion:** per-category quote/reserve; clear pricing tiers; delivery radius.

## 5. Shopify Store (Kyle's own DTC venture)

- **Anchor:** **E. Bold Commerce**, accent **derived from the product photography** (never purple).
- **Page job:** sell the product; reduce thinking between landing and checkout.
- **Hero:** oversized real product shot with type wrapping/overlapping the edge; single clear value prop; primary buy action.
- **Sections:** product clarity (what it is / who it's for) → real lifestyle imagery → benefits in plain terms → trust (reviews, returns, shipping, guarantees) → comparison if relevant → frictionless cart path → sticky add-to-cart that lifts on intent.
- **Copy voice:** brand-specific, confident, benefit-led, specific (not "premium quality solutions").
- **Conversion:** trust signals near the buy button; real reviews; clear shipping/returns; minimal-friction checkout; mobile-first.

## 6. Stannect's Own Brand & Products (agency site, KPI dashboard product)

- **Anchor:** **F. Editorial Tech** — calm, capable, data-forward, restrained.
- **Page job (agency site):** establish credibility for GHL/automation/integration work and book a call.
- **Page job (dashboard product):** show the product surface and the value (ad-spend joined to GHL outcomes — CPL/ROAS/CAC) and convert to demo/trial.
- **Hero:** a real/convincing product surface (annotated dashboard); metrics that count up because the number is the point.
- **Use Stannect's real brand tokens** (Kyle's existing hex palette, typography, logo CDN URLs from the Stannect primer) — *drop them in here when building* rather than the placeholder anchor values. Keep the anchor's restraint and spacing discipline.
- **Copy voice:** pragmatic, plain, results-oriented (matches Kyle's "ship and refine," friendly-conversational standard).
- **Conversion:** one clear path to a call/demo; proof (testimonials, real client outcomes) near the ask.

---

### Reminder for every preset
Start from the preset → load `DESIGN.md` → lock the anchor → pull 3–6 real references (Dribbble for feel, 21st.dev for structure) → write+critique tokens → build → clear the quality floor → remove one accessory. The preset removes the blank-page problem; it does **not** replace the decide-before-generate loop.
